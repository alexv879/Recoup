3. Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998

3.1 Statutory Rights

Under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 (as amended) ("Late Payment Act"), businesses in the UK have statutory rights to:

  • Claim interest on late payments at the Bank of England base rate plus 8% per annum
  • Claim fixed compensation for debt recovery costs:
    • £40 for debts under £1,000
    • £70 for debts between £1,000 and £9,999.99
    • £100 for debts of £10,000 or more
  • Recover reasonable costs of recovering the debt

3.2 Recoup's Late Payment Calculation Tools

Recoup provides automated calculation tools for late payment interest and statutory compensation in accordance with the Late Payment Act. These calculations are provided for informational purposes only.

3.3 User Responsibility

You are solely responsible for:

  • Verifying that your contract terms comply with the Late Payment Act
  • Ensuring payment terms in your invoices are enforceable
  • Confirming the accuracy of interest and compensation calculations
  • Determining when and how to pursue late payment claims
  • Complying with all applicable laws when recovering debts
Legal Reference: Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 (c. 20), as amended by the Late Payment of Commercial Debts Regulations 2013 (SI 2013/395). These provisions apply to commercial contracts for the supply of goods or services where both parties act in the course of business.
