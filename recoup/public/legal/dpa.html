Data Processing Agreement

Preamble

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between you ("Customer", "Data Controller") and Recoup Ltd ("Recoup", "Data Processor") and governs the processing of personal data on behalf of the Customer.

This DPA is entered into in compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

Legal Basis: Article 28 of the UK GDPR requires data processors to have a written contract with data controllers that sets out the subject matter, duration, nature, and purpose of processing, the type of personal data, and the categories of data subjects.

1. Definitions

The following terms have the meanings set out in the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018:

  • "Data Controller": The Customer who determines the purposes and means of processing personal data
  • "Data Processor": Recoup, which processes personal data on behalf of the Data Controller
  • "Personal Data": Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
  • "Processing": Any operation performed on personal data
  • "Data Subject": The individual to whom personal data relates
  • "Sub-processor": Any third party appointed by Recoup to process personal data
  • "UK GDPR": The UK General Data Protection Regulation
  • "Data Protection Laws": UK GDPR, Data Protection Act 2018, and PECR
  • "Personal Data Breach": A breach of security leading to accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorized disclosure of, or access to personal data

2. Scope and Roles

2.1 Controller-Processor Relationship

The parties acknowledge and agree that:

  • Customer is the Data Controller of personal data relating to Customer's clients and debtors
  • Recoup is the Data Processor of such personal data
  • Recoup is the Data Controller of personal data relating to Customer's account and usage
  • This DPA applies only to processing where Recoup acts as Data Processor

2.2 Subject Matter and Purpose

Subject Matter: Processing of personal data through the Recoup payment automation platform
Purpose: To provide invoice management, payment tracking, automated communications, and related services as described in the Terms of Service
Duration: For the term of the Customer's subscription and the data retention period thereafter

3. Details of Processing

3.1 Nature of Processing

Recoup will process personal data to provide the following services:

  • Storing and managing invoice and client information
  • Sending payment reminders via email, SMS, phone calls, and physical mail
  • Tracking payment status and generating reports
  • Calculating late payment interest and fees
  • Providing analytics and usage insights
  • Facilitating communication between Customer and their clients

3.2 Categories of Personal Data

Data Category Examples
Identification data Name, business name, company registration number
Contact data Email address, phone number, postal address
Financial data Invoice amounts, payment amounts, payment dates, bank details (if provided)
Transaction data Invoice details, payment history, late payment records
Communication data Email content, SMS messages, call records, physical mail content
Technical data IP addresses (of Customer's clients if applicable)

3.3 Categories of Data Subjects

  • Customer's business clients and debtors
  • Contact persons at client organizations
  • Individuals receiving payment reminders

3.4 Special Categories of Personal Data

Recoup does not require or expect to process special categories of personal data (as defined in Article 9 UK GDPR) or criminal conviction data. Customer must not upload such data without prior written agreement with Recoup.

4. Customer's Obligations as Data Controller

4.1 Lawful Processing

The Customer warrants that:

  • It has all necessary rights and lawful bases to process and share personal data with Recoup
  • It complies with all applicable Data Protection Laws
  • It has provided appropriate privacy notices to data subjects
  • Processing instructions given to Recoup comply with Data Protection Laws
  • It has obtained any necessary consents from data subjects

4.2 Processing Instructions

Customer instructs Recoup to process personal data:

  • In accordance with Data Protection Laws
  • As necessary to provide the Service described in the Terms of Service
  • As documented in this DPA
  • As further specified in Customer's use of the Service interface

4.3 Data Subject Rights

Customer is responsible for responding to requests from data subjects exercising their rights under UK GDPR (access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection). Recoup will provide reasonable assistance.

5. Recoup's Obligations as Data Processor

5.1 Processing Instructions

Recoup shall:

  • Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Customer
  • Not process personal data for Recoup's own purposes
  • Inform Customer if, in Recoup's opinion, an instruction violates Data Protection Laws
  • Immediately inform Customer if legally required to process data contrary to Customer's instructions

5.2 Confidentiality

Recoup shall:

  • Ensure persons authorized to process personal data are subject to confidentiality obligations
  • Maintain the confidentiality of personal data
  • Not disclose personal data to third parties except as authorized or required by law

5.3 Security Measures

Recoup implements appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:

  • Encryption:
    • Data encrypted in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
    • Data encrypted at rest using AES-256 or equivalent
  • Access Control:
    • Role-based access control (RBAC)
    • Multi-factor authentication for administrative access
    • Regular access reviews
    • Principle of least privilege
  • Data Integrity and Availability:
    • Regular automated backups
    • Disaster recovery and business continuity plans
    • Redundant infrastructure
  • Monitoring:
    • Security event logging and monitoring
    • Intrusion detection systems
    • Regular security assessments
  • Physical Security:
    • Data centers with physical access controls (provided by infrastructure partners)
    • 24/7 surveillance and monitoring
  • Organizational Measures:
    • Information security policies
    • Employee training on data protection
    • Incident response procedures
Technical and Organizational Measures - Detailed Annex
A detailed description of security measures is available in Annex 1: Security Measures (available upon request to enterprise customers).

5.4.2 Sub-processor Obligations

Recoup shall:

  • Impose data protection obligations on sub-processors equivalent to this DPA
  • Ensure sub-processors provide sufficient guarantees of compliance with Data Protection Laws
  • Remain fully liable to Customer for sub-processor performance
  • Conduct due diligence on sub-processors

5.4.3 Changes to Sub-processors

Recoup shall:

  • Maintain an up-to-date list of sub-processors at [recoup.com/legal/subprocessors]
  • Provide at least 30 days' notice before adding or replacing sub-processors
  • Provide Customer with opportunity to object to new sub-processors

If Customer objects on reasonable data protection grounds, the parties shall work together in good faith to find a solution. If no solution is found, Customer may terminate the affected services.

5.5 Data Subject Rights Assistance

Recoup shall:

  • Provide reasonable assistance to Customer in responding to data subject requests
  • Notify Customer if Recoup receives a direct request from a data subject (within 5 business days)
  • Not respond to data subject requests without Customer's prior authorization
  • Provide tools and interfaces to enable Customer to fulfill data subject rights (e.g., data export, deletion)

5.6 Data Protection Impact Assessments

Recoup shall provide reasonable assistance to Customer in conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) when required, including providing information about processing operations and security measures.

5.7 Personal Data Breaches

In the event of a personal data breach, Recoup shall:

  • Notify Customer without undue delay and no later than 48 hours after becoming aware
  • Provide Customer with sufficient information to meet Customer's breach notification obligations, including:
    • Nature of the breach
    • Categories and approximate number of data subjects and records affected
    • Likely consequences of the breach
    • Measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate harm
  • Cooperate with Customer's investigation and provide timely updates
  • Take reasonable steps to remediate the breach and prevent recurrence
Note: Customer remains responsible for determining whether to notify the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) and affected data subjects, in accordance with UK GDPR Articles 33-34.

5.8 Audits and Inspections

5.8.1 Audit Rights

Recoup shall:

  • Make available to Customer all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA
  • Allow for and contribute to audits conducted by Customer or Customer's auditor

5.8.2 Audit Process

Audits shall be conducted as follows:

  • Frequency: Maximum once per 12 months (unless breach or regulator request)
  • Notice: Minimum 30 days' advance written notice
  • Timing: During business hours, minimizing disruption
  • Scope: Limited to processing of Customer's personal data
  • Confidentiality: Auditors must sign Recoup's NDA
  • Cost: Customer bears audit costs; Recoup may charge reasonable fees for assistance exceeding 8 hours

5.8.3 Alternative Compliance Evidence

Recoup may satisfy audit requirements by providing:

  • SOC 2 Type II reports (when available)
  • ISO 27001 certification (when available)
  • Third-party security assessments
  • Summary audit reports

6. International Data Transfers

6.1 Data Storage

Personal data is primarily stored within the United Kingdom and European Economic Area (EEA).

6.2 Transfers Outside UK/EEA

Where personal data is transferred to countries outside the UK/EEA, Recoup ensures appropriate safeguards are in place:

  • US Transfers:
    • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) - UK International Data Transfer Agreement/Addendum
    • Supplementary measures including encryption, access controls, and transparency
    • Sub-processors' commitments to equivalent protections
  • Adequacy Decisions: Transfers to countries with UK adequacy decisions where applicable

6.3 Transfer Mechanisms

For transfers to sub-processors outside the UK/EEA, Recoup has implemented:

  • UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA) or Addendum to EU SCCs
  • Contractual obligations on sub-processors to comply with transfer requirements
  • Technical measures (encryption, pseudonymization) to protect data in transit and at rest
Transfer Impact Assessment
Recoup has conducted Transfer Impact Assessments (TIAs) for transfers to third countries, available in Annex 2: International Transfers (available upon request to enterprise customers).

7. Data Deletion and Return

7.1 Deletion upon Termination

Upon termination or expiry of the Terms of Service, Recoup shall, at Customer's choice:

  • Delete all personal data, or
  • Return all personal data to Customer in a structured, commonly used format

7.2 Deletion Timeline

  • Production Systems: Deleted within 30 days of termination
  • Backups: Deleted in accordance with standard backup rotation (maximum 90 days)
  • Exception: Data may be retained if required by applicable law, with notification to Customer

7.3 Certification of Deletion

Upon request, Recoup will provide written certification that personal data has been deleted or returned.

7.4 Data Retention for Legal Obligations

Recoup may retain personal data to the extent required by UK law (e.g., financial records for tax purposes). Such retained data will remain subject to confidentiality obligations and Data Protection Laws.

8. Liability and Indemnification

8.1 Liability Allocation

Each party shall be liable for its own breaches of Data Protection Laws:

  • Customer: Liable for lawful basis, privacy notices, data subject consents, and instructions given to Recoup
  • Recoup: Liable for processing in accordance with instructions, security measures, and sub-processor management

8.2 Limitation of Liability

Liability under this DPA is subject to the limitation of liability provisions in the Terms of Service, except where prohibited by Data Protection Laws.

8.3 Indemnification

Each party shall indemnify the other for losses arising from the indemnifying party's breach of Data Protection Laws, subject to:

  • Prompt written notice of the claim
  • Reasonable cooperation in defense
  • Sole control of defense and settlement (with consent not to be unreasonably withheld)

9. Term and Termination

9.1 Term

This DPA takes effect on the date Customer accepts the Terms of Service and remains in force for the duration of the Terms of Service or until all personal data is deleted or returned.

9.2 Termination

Either party may terminate this DPA:

  • If the other party materially breaches this DPA and fails to remedy within 30 days of notice
  • Upon termination of the Terms of Service
  • If required by Data Protection Laws or regulatory order

9.3 Survival

Obligations relating to data deletion, confidentiality, and liability survive termination.

10. General Provisions

10.1 Relationship to Terms of Service

This DPA is incorporated into and forms part of the Terms of Service. In case of conflict regarding data processing, this DPA prevails.

10.2 Order of Precedence

  1. This Data Processing Agreement
  2. Terms of Service
  3. Other policies and guidelines

10.3 Amendments

Recoup may update this DPA to reflect:

  • Changes in Data Protection Laws
  • Regulatory guidance
  • Changes to sub-processors (with notice as specified)
  • Improvements to security measures

Material changes will be communicated with 30 days' notice. Continued use of the Service after the effective date constitutes acceptance.

10.4 Severability

If any provision of this DPA is held invalid or unenforceable, it shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it valid, or severed if modification is not possible, without affecting the remainder.

10.5 Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This DPA is governed by the laws of England and Wales. Disputes shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
