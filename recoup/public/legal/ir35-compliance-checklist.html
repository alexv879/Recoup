IR35 Compliance Checklist for Recoup Users

⚠️ Important Disclaimer: This checklist is provided for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal or tax advice. IR35 status is complex and depends on your specific circumstances. Recoup recommends consulting a qualified accountant or IR35 specialist for personalized advice.

Recoup's Role: Recoup is a software tool for managing invoices and payments. Using Recoup does not determine your IR35 status. Your tax status depends on the nature of your working relationship with your clients, not the tools you use for invoicing.

1. What is IR35?

1.1 Overview

IR35 is UK tax legislation (officially called "off-payroll working rules") designed to combat tax avoidance by workers supplying their services through an intermediary (usually a limited company or partnership) who would be employees if the intermediary were not used.

Legal Framework:
  • Original Legislation: Finance Act 2000, Chapter 8
  • Private Sector Reform: Finance Act 2017 (public sector), Finance Act 2021 (medium/large private sector)
  • Governing Body: HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC)
  • Official Tool: HMRC Check Employment Status for Tax (CEST) tool

1.2 Who Does IR35 Apply To?

Scenario IR35 Applies? Responsibility
Public sector contractor Yes Client determines status (since 2017)
Private sector - Medium/Large client (£10.2m+ turnover, 50+ employees, £5.1m+ balance sheet) Yes Client determines status (since April 2021)
Private sector - Small client Yes Contractor determines own status
Working overseas (outside UK) Potentially - complex Depends on tax residency and location of work
Sole trader (no intermediary) No Self-employed tax rules apply

1.3 IR35 Status Outcomes

  • "Inside IR35" (Deemed Employment):
    • You're treated as an employee for tax purposes
    • Income tax and National Insurance deducted at source (if client determines status)
    • Or you must operate PAYE through your company (if you determine status)
    • Cannot claim as many business expenses
  • "Outside IR35" (Genuinely Self-Employed):
    • You pay corporation tax on company profits
    • Can draw salary and dividends tax-efficiently
    • Can claim legitimate business expenses
    • More administrative flexibility

2. Key IR35 Tests

HMRC and courts assess employment status using three main tests and additional factors:

2.1 The Three Primary Tests

1. Control

Question: Does the client control what you do, how you do it, when you do it, and where you do it?

Outside IR35 indicators: You decide how to complete work, set your own hours, work from your own location, use your own methods/tools.

Inside IR35 indicators: Client sets your hours, location, directs your methods, requires presence at client site, manages you like an employee.

2. Substitution (Personal Service)

Question: Can you send someone else to do the work in your place?

Outside IR35 indicators: Contract allows substitution, you have genuinely exercised this right or could do so, client accepts substitutes.

Inside IR35 indicators: You must personally perform the work, no substitution allowed, client hired you specifically for your personal skills.

3. Mutuality of Obligation (MOO)

Question: Is the client obligated to offer you work, and are you obligated to accept it?

Outside IR35 indicators: Project-based work, no guarantee of ongoing work, you can refuse work without consequences.

Inside IR35 indicators: Continuous work relationship, expectation of ongoing work, obligation to accept work offered.

2.2 Additional Factors

Factor Outside IR35 (Self-Employed) Inside IR35 (Employee-Like)
Financial Risk You bear financial risk, can make profit/loss, invest in business Paid fixed rate regardless of outcomes, no financial risk
Equipment Provide own equipment, tools, software Client provides equipment and tools
Part and Parcel Not integrated into organization, clearly external contractor Treated like employee, included in org structure, given company email/badge
Exclusivity Work for multiple clients simultaneously Exclusive or near-exclusive to one client
Benefits No employee benefits (pension, sick pay, holiday pay) Receive benefits similar to employees
Contracts Fixed-term or project-based contracts Rolling or indefinite contracts
Termination Notice Short or no notice period (contractual) Long notice periods (like employees)

3. IR35 Compliance Checklist

How to Use This Checklist:
  1. Answer honestly based on your actual working arrangements, not what your contract says
  2. Consider each client engagement separately - status may vary
  3. More "Yes" answers indicate stronger outside IR35 position
  4. This is a guide only - use HMRC's CEST tool and consult a professional

Contract and Documentation

Your contract explicitly states you're providing services as an independent contractor, not as an employee.

You have the contractual right to send a substitute, and this right is real (not just on paper).

Contract has clear start and end dates, or is tied to specific deliverables/projects.

No exclusivity clause preventing you from working for multiple clients at once.

Control and Independence

Client tells you what needs to be achieved, but you decide how to do it.

You're not required to work specific hours or be available at set times.

You can work from home, your office, or other locations - not required to be at client site.

Client doesn't manage you day-to-day like an employee; you work autonomously.

Not expected to attend team meetings, training sessions, or company events.

Financial Risk and Reward

If project takes longer than expected or has issues, you bear the cost - not paid hourly.

Paid for outcomes/deliverables, not just time spent.

Use your own computer, software licenses, office space, phone, etc.

Pay for professional development, insurance, software, office costs, etc.

You carry business insurance to cover professional risks.

Business Structure and Operations

You actively work for multiple clients, or actively market to new clients.

You operate as a business with professional branding and marketing.

Formally registered with Companies House (if limited company) and HMRC.

You submit invoices for payment, not timesheets to payroll department.

You set payment terms and chase late payments (Recoup helps with this!).

Integration and Culture

Not listed on org chart, not included in team hierarchy.

Client doesn't provide any employment benefits.

You use your own business email; client access is minimal and project-specific.

Employee handbooks and HR policies don't apply to you.

Client's staff know you're a contractor, not an employee.

Substitution and Personal Service

Contract allows it and client would accept a suitable substitute you provide.

You've sent a substitute before, or have people identified who could step in.

Client hired your company for expertise, not you personally.

Mutuality of Obligation

Contract is for specific project(s); no guarantee of future work.

You can turn down new projects or additional tasks without consequences.

Work is project-based with clear starts/ends, not continuous rolling contracts.

4. Risk Assessment

Scoring Guide

Count how many boxes you checked above:

  • 25-36 boxes: Lower IR35 Risk - Strong indicators of self-employment
  • 15-24 boxes: Medium IR35 Risk - Mixed indicators; seek professional advice
  • 0-14 boxes: Higher IR35 Risk - Many employment indicators; likely inside IR35

Important: This is an informal guide only. Even one strong employment indicator (e.g., lack of substitution) can place you inside IR35. Always use the official HMRC CEST tool and consult an IR35 specialist.

5. Using Recoup While Managing IR35 Status

5.1 Recoup is IR35-Neutral

Using Recoup to manage invoices and payments does not affect your IR35 status. IR35 is determined by your working relationship with clients, not the administrative tools you use.

5.2 How Recoup Can Support Outside IR35 Status

IR35 Factor How Recoup Helps
Professional Invoicing Create professional invoices showing your business details, reinforcing contractor status
Payment Terms Set and enforce your own payment terms (e.g., 30 days), not client payroll terms
Business Operations Automated systems show you run a business, not just work as disguised employee
Multiple Clients Manage invoices for multiple clients, demonstrating diverse client base
Late Payment Rights Exercise Late Payment Act rights (statutory interest & compensation) - employees can't do this
Record Keeping Maintain professional records for HMRC compliance and potential IR35 investigations

5.3 Best Practices for Recoup Users

  • Invoice from Your Company: Ensure invoices show your limited company/business name, not personal name
  • Include Company Details: Company registration number, VAT number (if registered), registered address
  • Professional Payment Terms: Set clear payment terms (30 days, etc.) on all invoices
  • Track Multiple Clients: Use Recoup to manage invoices for all clients, showing diversified business
  • Apply Late Payment Interest: Use Recoup's late payment calculation features when appropriate
  • Maintain Records: Keep all invoice and payment records for at least 6 years (HMRC requirement)

6. What to Do If You're Inside IR35

6.1 Client Determines Status (Medium/Large Private Sector, Public Sector)

If your client determines you're inside IR35:

  • Client (or fee-payer) will deduct income tax and National Insurance at source
  • You'll receive a Status Determination Statement (SDS)
  • You can challenge the determination if you disagree
  • Still invoice through your company, but net of tax/NI deductions
  • Can still use Recoup for invoicing and tracking

6.2 You Determine Status (Small Private Sector Clients)

If you determine you're inside IR35:

  • Operate PAYE through your company for income from that contract
  • Deduct income tax and employee/employer National Insurance
  • Pay yourself a salary (subject to PAYE) from the contract income
  • Consider using an accountant to ensure compliance
  • Keep detailed records
  • Can still use Recoup for client invoicing

7. HMRC CEST Tool

7.1 What is CEST?

Check Employment Status for Tax (CEST) is HMRC's official online tool for determining IR35 status.

Access CEST: www.gov.uk/guidance/check-employment-status-for-tax

7.2 Using CEST

  • Complete for each client engagement separately
  • Answer based on actual working practices, not just contract terms
  • Be honest - HMRC can challenge inaccurate assessments
  • Save the result and determination reference
  • Review periodically, especially if working arrangements change

7.3 CEST Limitations

Note: CEST doesn't cover all scenarios and has limitations:
  • Doesn't work well for complex arrangements
  • May give "unable to determine" result
  • Not legally binding, but HMRC says they'll stand by result if you answer honestly
  • Consider professional advice if result is unclear

8. Professional Advice and Resources

8.1 When to Seek Professional Help

Consult an IR35 specialist or accountant if:

  • Your CEST result is unclear or "unable to determine"
  • Your client has determined you inside IR35 and you disagree
  • Your working arrangements are complex or unusual
  • You work for multiple clients with different statuses
  • You're concerned about an HMRC investigation
  • You're considering challenging an inside IR35 determination
  • You want to restructure contracts to improve IR35 position

8.2 Professional Bodies and Resources

  • HMRC IR35 Guidance: www.gov.uk/guidance/understanding-off-payroll-working-ir35
  • HMRC Helpline: 0300 200 3500

10. Document Retention

For IR35 compliance, retain the following for at least 6 years:

  • All client contracts and statements of work
  • Status Determination Statements (SDS) from clients
  • CEST assessment results
  • All invoices and payment records (Recoup helps with this!)
  • Correspondence about working arrangements
  • Evidence of substitution rights or use
  • Records of other clients and business activities
  • Business expense records
  • Professional advice received
