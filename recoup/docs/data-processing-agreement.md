## 1. Definitions

- **Personal Data:** Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
- **Processing:** Any operation performed on Personal Data (collection, storage, use, transfer, deletion)
- **Data Subject:** The individual to whom Personal Data relates
- **Controller:** The entity determining the purposes and means of processing (the Client)
- **Processor:** The entity processing data on behalf of the Controller (us)
- **Subprocessor:** A third party engaged by the Processor to process data
- **Data Breach:** Unauthorized or accidental destruction, loss, alteration, or disclosure of Personal Data
- **GDPR:** General Data Protection Regulation (UK/EU)

## 5. Processor's Obligations

### 5.1 Processing Instructions
The Processor shall process Personal Data only:
- In accordance with documented instructions from the Controller
- For the purposes specified in this DPA
- In compliance with UK GDPR and applicable data protection laws

The Processor shall inform the Controller if an instruction infringes GDPR or other applicable law.

### 5.2 Confidentiality
The Processor shall ensure that all employees and subprocessors with access to Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.

### 5.3 Data Security
The Processor implements and maintains appropriate technical and organizational security measures, including:
- Encryption in transit (SSL/TLS) and at rest (AES-256)
- Access controls (authentication, authorization, role-based access)
- Regular security audits and vulnerability assessments
- Intrusion detection and monitoring
- Incident response procedures
- Employee training on data protection

**Security Standard:** The Processor implements security measures appropriate to the risk level of the Personal Data processed, consistent with industry standards.

### 5.4 Sub-processors
The Processor shall not engage subprocessors without prior written authorization from the Controller. A list of approved subprocessors is maintained in Schedule B.

The Processor shall:
- Notify the Controller in advance of any subprocessor changes
- Allow the Controller 30 days to object to new subprocessors
- Ensure subprocessors are bound by written data protection obligations no less protective than this DPA

### 5.5 Data Subject Rights
The Processor shall assist the Controller in fulfilling Data Subject rights requests, including:
- Right of Access (DSAR): Provide a copy of Personal Data within 10 business days of request
- Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
- Right to Erasure: Delete data upon request (except where retention is legally required)
- Right to Restrict Processing: Limit processing upon request
- Right to Data Portability: Export data in a structured, portable format (JSON, CSV)
- Right to Object: Honor objections to processing for marketing/analytics

### 5.6 Data Subject Requests
Upon request from the Controller, the Processor shall:
- Assist in locating and compiling Personal Data
- Export data in a machine-readable format within 10 business days
- Provide documentation of processing activities

The Processor may charge a reasonable fee for requests exceeding one per quarter.

### 5.7 Audit & Compliance
The Processor shall:
- Maintain records of processing activities (ROPA)
- Provide evidence of GDPR compliance upon request
- Grant the Controller reasonable access for audit/inspection
- Cooperate with data protection authorities (ICO) during investigations
- Provide an annual security certification or SOC 2 report

## 6. Controller's Obligations

### 6.1 Lawful Processing
The Controller warrants that:
- It has a lawful basis for processing Personal Data (contract, consent, legal obligation, etc.)
- It has obtained necessary consents/notices for Data Subjects
- Personal Data provided to the Processor is accurate and lawful

### 6.2 Instructions
The Controller shall:
- Provide clear, documented processing instructions
- Inform the Processor of changes to processing purposes or data types
- Ensure instructions comply with applicable law

### 6.3 Responsibility
The Controller remains responsible for:
- Compliance with GDPR (data protection principles, lawfulness, transparency, etc.)
- Responding to Data Subject requests
- Managing Data Subject rights
- Compliance with data protection impact assessments (DPIAs)

## 7. Data Transfers & Locations

### 7.1 Processing Locations
Personal Data will be processed and stored in the following locations:
- **Primary:** UK
- **Backup/Disaster Recovery:** EU data centers
- **Subprocessors:** May process data in US/other jurisdictions (see Schedule B)

### 7.2 International Transfers
Where Personal Data is transferred outside the UK/EEA (e.g., to US subprocessors), the Processor ensures appropriate safeguards:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) are in place with all subprocessors
- Data Transfer Impact Assessments (DTIAs) are conducted
- Additional technical measures (encryption, access controls) are implemented

The Processor shall notify the Controller of any restriction or suspension of data flows due to legal/regulatory changes.

---

## 8. Data Breach & Incident Management

### 8.1 Notification
The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay upon discovery of a Personal Data Breach. Notification shall include:
- Nature and scope of the breach
- Categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected
- Likely consequences
- Measures taken or proposed to mitigate harm
- Contact information for further inquiries

**Target Notification Timeline:** Within 24 hours of discovery (or as soon as reasonably practicable)

### 8.2 Cooperation
The Processor shall:
- Cooperate with the Controller in breach investigations
- Preserve evidence and forensic materials
- Assist with regulatory notifications (ICO)
- Assist with Data Subject notifications (if required)

### 8.3 Liability
The Processor's liability for breaches caused by its own negligence is covered under the underlying Services Agreement and applicable law.

---

## 9. Data Deletion & Return

### 9.1 Upon Termination
Upon termination or expiration of the Services Agreement, the Processor shall:
- Delete or return all Personal Data at the Controller's written instruction
- Certify deletion in writing
- Ensure deletion occurs within 30 days (or as required by law)

**Exception:** The Processor may retain Personal Data where required by law (e.g., invoices for 7 years for tax compliance) or where technical/legal constraints prevent deletion.

### 9.2 Backups
Personal Data retained in automated backups shall be deleted according to the Processor's backup retention policy (typically 90 days).
