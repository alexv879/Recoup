## üìö PRIMARY LEGISLATION

### Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998
**Purpose:** Gives businesses statutory right to charge interest on late payments

**Key Provisions:**
- Automatic right to 8% + Bank of England base rate interest
- Fixed compensation (¬£40-¬£100) for debt recovery costs
- Applies to B2B transactions after 7 August 2002
- Interest starts day after due date

**Full Text:** https://www.legislation.gov.uk/ukpga/1998/20/contents

---

### Late Payment of Commercial Debts Regulations 2002 (as amended 2013)
**Purpose:** Implements EU Late Payment Directive, sets procedures

**Key Amendments (2013):**
- 30-day maximum payment terms (public sector)
- 60-day maximum payment terms (private sector, unless explicitly agreed)
- Enhanced creditor rights for SMEs

**Full Text:** https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2013/395/contents/made

---

### Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Rate of Interest) (No. 3) Order 2002
**Purpose:** Sets statutory interest rate calculation method

**Provisions:**
- Interest rate = 8% + "reference rate" (BoE base rate)
- Reference rate determined by 30 June or 31 December before payment overdue
- Rate changes twice yearly (1 January and 1 July)

**Full Text:** https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2002/1675/contents/made

## ‚öñÔ∏è COUNTY COURT CLAIMS

### Money Claim Online (MCOL)
**URL:** https://www.moneyclaim.gov.uk

**Service Description:**
- File court claims online (up to ¬£100,000)
- Track claim progress
- Respond to claims
- Request default judgment
- Apply for enforcement

**Requirements:**
- Government Gateway account
- Claim details (amount, debtor info, evidence)
- Court fee payment (debit/credit card)

**Timeline:**
1. File claim online (15-30 minutes)
2. Court serves claim on debtor (5-7 days)
3. Debtor has 14 days to respond
4. If no response: Default judgment entered
5. If disputed: Court hearing scheduled (8-12 weeks)

### Letter Before Action Template
Required before County Court claim (14-day notice)

```
[Your Business Letterhead]
[Date]

TO: [Debtor Name]
[Debtor Address]

LETTER BEFORE ACTION - OUTSTANDING DEBT

Re: Invoice [Number], Amount: ¬£[X,XXX.XX]

Dear [Name],

I am writing to inform you that despite previous reminders, the above invoice
remains unpaid and is now [X] days overdue.

DEBT SUMMARY:
- Invoice Number: [INV-XXX]
- Invoice Date: [Date]
- Due Date: [Date]
- Original Amount: ¬£[X,XXX.XX]
- Days Overdue: [X days]

STATUTORY CHARGES:
Under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998, I am entitled to:
- Statutory Interest: ¬£[XXX.XX] (13.25% p.a., accruing daily)
- Fixed Compensation: ¬£[40/70/100]
- TOTAL NOW OWED: ¬£[X,XXX.XX]

PAYMENT DEADLINE: 14 DAYS FROM DATE OF THIS LETTER

Payment must be received by [Date] to avoid County Court proceedings.

CONSEQUENCES OF NON-PAYMENT:
If payment is not received within 14 days, I will issue a County Court claim
without further notice. This will result in:
1. County Court Judgment (CCJ) against you/your business
2. Additional court fees (¬£[XXX]) added to the debt
3. Damage to your credit rating
4. Possible enforcement action (bailiffs, asset seizure)

PAYMENT DETAILS:
[Bank details]
Reference: [Invoice Number]

If you dispute this debt or require a payment arrangement, you must respond in
writing within 5 days.

Yours sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
```
