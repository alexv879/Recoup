Cookie Policy

1. Introduction

1.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

1.2 Why We Use Cookies

Recoup uses cookies to:

  • Keep you signed in to your account
  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Understand how you use our Service
  • Improve your experience
  • Ensure the security of our platform
  • Analyze usage patterns and performance

1.3 Legal Basis

Legal Framework: This Cookie Policy complies with:
  • UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) - Article 6 (lawful basis for processing)
  • Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) 2003 - Regulation 6 (cookies and similar technologies)
  • Data Protection Act 2018

4. Cookie Duration

4.1 Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary and are deleted when you close your browser. We use them for:

  • Maintaining your logged-in state during a browsing session
  • Security and fraud prevention
  • Temporary preferences

4.2 Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. We use them for:

  • Remembering you when you return (if you chose "Remember me")
  • Storing your cookie preferences
  • Analytics and performance monitoring

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

5.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner allowing you to:

  • Accept All: Consent to all cookie types
  • Reject Non-Essential: Allow only strictly necessary cookies
  • Customize: Choose which categories you consent to
Update Your Preferences: You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking below:

Manage Cookie Preferences

5.2 Browser Settings

You can also control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View and delete cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Delete cookies when you close the browser
  • Set exceptions for specific websites

How to Manage Cookies in Popular Browsers:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Cookies and site data

For detailed instructions, visit: www.aboutcookies.org or www.allaboutcookies.org

Warning: Blocking all cookies may prevent you from using certain features of the Service, including logging in and accessing your account.

6. Do Not Track (DNT) Signals

Some browsers offer "Do Not Track" (DNT) settings. Currently, there is no industry standard for responding to DNT signals. We do not specifically respond to DNT browser signals.

However, you can control cookies through:

  • Our cookie consent banner
  • Your browser settings
  • Third-party opt-out tools

7. Mobile Identifiers

If you access Recoup through a mobile app (when available), we may use mobile advertising identifiers similar to cookies:

  • iOS: Identifier for Advertisers (IDFA)
  • Android: Google Advertising ID (GAID)

You can opt out of targeted advertising on mobile:

  • iOS: Settings > Privacy > Tracking > "Allow Apps to Request to Track" (disable)
